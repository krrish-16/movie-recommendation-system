01
The Data preprocess.py
We start with two spreadsheets — one listing movies, one listing who rated what. We merge them into a giant table called the User-Movie Matrix. Each row is a user, each column is a movie, each cell is their rating. Zero means they haven't seen it.

02
Finding Similar Users model.py
Each user's ratings form a vector — like a direction they're pointing in space. We measure the angle between two users' vectors. A small angle = similar taste. This is called Cosine Similarity. We find your 20 most similar "neighbours."

03
Grouping Users clustering.py
K-Means automatically sorts all 500 users into 5 taste groups — without anyone telling it what the groups mean. It discovers patterns like "these 99 people all tend to like similar movies." Think of it like sorting people at a party by music taste, automatically.
HOW K-MEANS WORKS
Step 1 — Place 5 random centre points
Step 2 — Assign each user to nearest centre
Step 3 — Move centres to middle of their group
Step 4 — Repeat until groups stop changing
Result — 5 stable clusters of similar users ✓
04
Pattern Mining apriori.py
The Apriori algorithm scans all ratings to find "if a user liked X, they also liked Y" patterns. Like how supermarkets noticed people who buy bread also buy butter. We apply the same logic to movies. Each rule has a confidence (how often it's true) and a lift (how much better than random).
